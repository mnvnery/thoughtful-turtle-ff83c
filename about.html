Márquez ensures audiences form a close rapport with the protagonist", was the highlight given by Variety to his work in the Brazilian family drama "Como Nossos País", directed by Laís Bodanzky and premiered in the Panorama Section of the Berlin International Film Festival in 2017.

The ability to adapt to different proposals and, at the same time, achieve a connection with the most intimate core of each story is, precisely, one of the challenges that Pedro always pursues in his work.

In "Ex-Shaman", by Luiz Bolognesi, a film about the ethnocide suffered by an indigenous community in Amazonas, his work once again attracts Variety's attention: “What’s not conjectural is the photographic beauty of the widescreen images, shot by Pedro J. Márquez with a timeless stillness that seems to resist the race toward change that began in 1969 and has caused so much destruction ever since". The movie won a Special Mention at the Berlinale in 2018, among other international awards.

After the relevance of his work in his first movie, "Kidnapped" (2011), by Miguel A. Vivas, a thriller filmed in 12 shots in 12 days of shooting for which Pedro was nominated for the Best Cinematographer Debut at Camerimage; Ryuhei Kitamura trusted him the Japanese blockbuster "Lupin The Third", a live action of the homonymous manga. The movie was a Box Office success in Japan where achieved more than 23 million dollars.

Pedro’s next project was in Cuba, shooting with Felix Viscarret the movie "Vientos de La Havana", and the mini series for Netflix "Four Seasons in La Havana" (Platino Award for Best Ibero-American Series). Also with Viscarret, he shot "Saura (s)", a documentary about the legendary Spanish filmmaker, Carlos Saura. The film was honored with a Best Documentary nomination at the Goya Awards.
